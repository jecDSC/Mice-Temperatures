Design Decisions

The color of the respective temperature lines is deliberate, many people associate the color blue with male and pink with female. Additionally, each portion of the graph displays whether the female mice are in the estrus period or not by highlighting the portions where they are with gray, and leaving the rest of the background unchanged. This is so that viewers of the plot would be able to compare the differences in temperatures between estrus days and normal days with just a look, without needing to know context about the dataset.

We also implemented an interactive element: tooltips. Users will be able to highlight a portion of the graph that they want more details on, displaying the average body temperatures over the duration, the time, if the lights are on, and status on ovulation. Additionally, tooltips will allow for the user to click on any part of the lineplot they want and see the value of the average temperature of all mice of that gender in the hour, the time, if the lights are on or off, and the status on ovulation.

Development Process

We met as three to discuss the project and our ideas for it initially, with all three of us putting in equal work to get to that idea. We then divided our efforts: Jeremy worked mostly on the visualization, Elvin worked on the styling of the site, and Vincent worked mostly on the site and the writeup. We spent around 2 hours each on this project, with the most time consuming portion being the planning and brainstorming at the beginning.
